What we've learned — Business

The wholesale buyer has changed. Ben-Ami's commerce model needs to catch up.

The buyers Ben-Ami sells to — body shop owners, detail shop managers, parts managers — are the same people who order on Amazon at home. They've stopped tolerating phone-and-text ordering, paper invoices, and waiting on a rep to confirm pricing. Steven sees it directly in the revenue mix: every covered SKU that shows up on Amazon is one Ben-Ami is no longer earning.

Trend 01
B2B buyers expect Amazon-grade self-service
"Make your system like Amazon's system" — Steven, in his own words. Search, account-aware pricing, 24/7 access, and scan-to-order are the table-stakes the rep model can't deliver.
Trend 02
Channel leakage to consumer marketplaces is accelerating
Top 20 accounts reorder weekly or bi-weekly on tight cycles. When the rep is the bottleneck, those buyers route to Amazon for the SKUs Ben-Ami already carries — and the relationship erodes one order at a time.
Trend 03
ERP-led commerce is the model going forward
Avacon's Business Central rollout is 90–95% complete. Steven and Matthew have built the system of record first, and now need the buyer-facing layer to plug into it — not duplicate it.
Where Ben-Ami is going
A self-service buying portal as the front door, reps in a higher-leverage role placing complex orders inside customer accounts, and the back office freed from manual order entry and AR collections. The lost year on the abandoned BigCommerce build has reset the timing — and made the September 1 go-live ahead of BFCM non-negotiable.
